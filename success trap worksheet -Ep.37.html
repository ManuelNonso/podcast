"You cannot live in full expression while performing a version of yourself built around a fear you have never examined. The moment you trace that fear to its root and see it clearly — it loses the power it had when it was invisible."

In the episode that accompanies this worksheet, we introduced a pattern that appears behind the facade of more successful people than most would expect. The bigger the visible achievement, the more isolated the private fear. This worksheet takes you through the full coaching process — three layers of work that surface the fear, trace it to where it actually came from, and help you separate it from your identity so that it stops making decisions on your behalf.

This is not a quick exercise. Set aside 60 to 90 minutes. Find a quiet space. Bring honesty — not the edited version of yourself, but the full one.

📋 How to Use This Worksheet

1

Work in order. Each section builds on the last. Do not skip ahead — the depth of Part 3 depends on the honesty of Part 1.

2

Write everything. Not in your head. The act of writing makes the invisible visible — and the invisible is exactly what we are working with today.

3

Share one answer with someone you trust when you are done. A spoken commitment holds in a way a private one never quite does.

You cannot work on what you cannot see. This section is about bringing the fear into the light — not to judge it, but to name it. Most successful people have never said this out loud to anyone. By the end of this section, you will have said it to yourself.

🌱 Part 2: Trace It — Finding the Root

In the episode, we shared the story of a client who traced her fear of being seen all the way back to watching her mother's business fail — and the fear her mother carried, which was passed on without either of them realising it. This section helps you trace your own fear to its root. Most people have never done this work. The ones who have describe it as the most clarifying experience of their professional lives.

The Cold Stove Principle

You May Be Flinching Near a Danger That No Longer Exists

A child who watches a parent burn their hand on a hot stove will flinch near stoves for years — even ones that are completely cold. The warning was real once. The pain was real once. And so the body remembers — protecting automatically, without asking whether the current stove is actually dangerous.

Many of the fears high performers carry work exactly this way. The fear was inherited — passed on with love, with good intention, from someone who wanted to protect. But their stove and your stove are not the same. Their time, their circumstances, their capabilities were not yours.

The exercises in this section help you find the stove — the original source of the warning — so you can examine it clearly, honour it, and choose consciously whether to keep flinching near it.

Root Exercise 1 of 3

When Did You First Learn That Success Was Dangerous?

Not in those words. Nobody said it in those words. But somewhere, at some point — possibly a very long time ago — you absorbed a message that told you reaching for something and falling short was worse than not reaching at all. Or that visibility brought judgment. Or that the higher you climb, the further you fall. When did you first receive that message?

Coaching Question

"Think back — before your career, before your business, before you built any of what you have now. What did you watch someone you loved experience that taught you to be careful about success, visibility, or failure?"

This might be a parent, a sibling, a mentor, or a teacher. It might be something you witnessed, something that was said, or something that was communicated through silence and avoidance. Take your time.

✂ Part 3: Separate It — Distinguishing the Fear From You

This is where the real liberation happens. You have named the fear. You have traced it to its root. Now the work is to separate it from your identity — to distinguish between the story the fear has been telling and the evidence your own life is actually offering you every single day.

Separation Exercise 1 of 3

The Evidence Your Life Is Already Offering You

The fear has been rehearsing its case against you for years. It has been collecting every moment of doubt, every difficult day, every client that did not renew, every piece of content that underperformed — and using all of it as evidence that it is right. Your actual evidence deserves the same floor.

Coaching Question

"What evidence in your own life directly contradicts the fear you have been carrying? Not what you hope might be true. What is already true — the messages, the results, the moments, the people who came back?"

Write everything. Do not edit for modesty. This is not a list for anyone else — it is the case for the defence against a fear that has been prosecuting you for years.

🔒 Part 4: Protect It — Keeping the Fear From Running the Show

Naming and tracing the fear is transformative. But transformation is not a single moment. It is a practice. This section gives you the ongoing structure to keep what you have discovered alive — so that the fear does not quietly reassert itself the moment the worksheet is closed.

📜 Your Full Expression Commitment

This is a written record of the work you have done today and the person you have decided to be on the other side of it. Write it carefully. Mean every word.

Your Ongoing Practice

How to Keep This Work Alive

The fear did not build in a day. It will not be fully dismantled in a single session. What you have done today is the most important step — naming it and tracing it. What you do next determines whether the insight stays or fades.

🗓 Weekly Check

Five minutes. One honest question:

Did I make a decision this week from the fear — or from my evidence?
Where did I manage the distance between public and private this week?
Where did I show up as the whole version of myself?
📆 Monthly Review

Twenty minutes. Revisit the evidence table:

What new evidence has appeared this month?
Is the fear quieter or louder than last month?
What is the one area where managed distance is still operating?
📅 Quarterly Reset

Sixty minutes. Return to this worksheet:

Retake the visibility fear scale in Part 1
Add new evidence to the table in Part 3
Rewrite the new story — let it grow with you
Final Reflection

The Full Expression Question

Before you close this worksheet, take your time with this final question. Do not rush it. Write as much as comes naturally.

The Question That Closes Every Great Coaching Session

"If you were no longer carrying this fear — if it was fully named, traced, and put down — who would you be? How would you show up in your work, your content, your coaching, your relationships? What would you attempt that you have been holding back? What would full expression actually look and feel like for you — not someday, but starting from this week?"

Write freely. This is your north star — the version of you this work is building toward.

Write your answer here. Be specific. Be honest. Be expansive.
